# AutoBookr Follow-Up Sequence

Five-stage outreach sequence over 21 days. Designed for persistence without being pushy—build relationship, add value, then gracefully exit or nurture.

---

## Sequence Overview

| Day | Stage | Touchpoint | Goal |
|-----|-------|------------|------|
| 1 | Initial Outreach | Email | Introduce AutoBookr with value prop |
| 3 | Friendly Check-In | Email | Gentle reminder, no hard sell |
| 7 | Value-Add | Email | Share case study or useful tip |
| 14 | Final Attempt | Email | One last try with incentive |
| 21 | Exit/Nurture | Email | Remove from active list or move to nurture |

---

## Day 1: Initial Outreach Email

**Timing:** Send Tuesday–Thursday, 7–9am

**Goal:** Introduce AutoBookr, highlight the problem (no-shows), offer solution.

### Subject Line Options

- A quick question about your booking headaches
- [Trade]s in [Town/City]—reclaim your lost hours
- How [Similar Trade] in [Town] stopped the no-shows

### Email Copy

Use one of the three templates from `outreach-email-templates.md`:

- **Template 1:** Friendly & Trust-Building (relationship-first)
- **Template 2:** Direct & Value-Focused (lead with numbers)
- **Template 3:** Social Proof (peer validation)

### Call to Action

- Reply to book a 5–10 minute demo: [calendly_link]
- Or simply reply "interested" and I'll send a link

### Success Criteria

- [ ] Email sent with personalized trade/location
- [ ] Clear CTA to book demo or reply
- [ ] Added to tracking spreadsheet (Name, Trade, Location, Date Sent)

## Day 3: Follow-Up If No Response

**Timing:** Day 3 (if no reply to Day 1), send Friday 10am or Monday 9am

**Goal:** Gentle check-in. Remind them you exist without being pushy.

### Subject Line Options

- Just checking in—no rush
- Quick follow-up on my last email
- Did you get a chance to look at this?

### Email Copy

```
Hi [First Name],

Hope you're having a good week—just wanted to check in briefly on my last email.

I know you're busy fixing things rather than sorting your inbox, so no worries if now's not the right time.

If you're curious about AutoBookr or have any questions, happy to chat. Even just a quick "not interested" so I stop emailing you is fine 😊

Book a time that works here: [calendly_link]

Cheers,
[Your Name]
```

### Call to Action

- Book a demo
- Reply "not interested" (easy out)
- Reply with questions

### Success Criteria

- [ ] Sent Day 3 if no reply received
- [ ] Tone is friendly, not pushy
- [ ] Provides easy "no" option to reduce guilt

## Day 7: Value-Add Follow-Up

**Timing:** Day 7, send Tuesday–Thursday 7–9am

**Goal:** Share something useful—a relevant case study or tip. Prove value without asking for anything.

### Subject Line Options

- A case study you might find interesting
- Quick tip that saved another [trade] 3 hours this week
- How [Similar Trade] in [Town] fixed his no-show problem

### Email Copy

**Option A: Case Study Focus**


### Call to Action

- Read the case study (link)

### Success Criteria

- [ ] Value-add content (not just "please book a call")
- [ ] Specific to their trade where possible
- [ ] Case study or tip is relevant to their pain point

## Day 14: Final Outreach (With Incentive)

**Timing:** Day 14, send Thursday 8am

**Goal:** Last-ditch effort with incentive. Make it easy to say yes.

### Subject Line Options

- Last one from me on this (quick offer)
- One final offer—[trade]s in [area] pilot
- Before I stop emailing you...

### Email Copy

```
Hi [First Name],

This is my last email on this one—I promise I won't bug you after this!

I've genuinely enjoyed learning a bit about what you're working on, and I think AutoBookr could help with the no-show problem. But I also know you're busy running your business, and this might not be the right time.

Here's my final offer:

**Free pilot for you—full AutoBookr system, zero cost, zero commitment.**

Why free? We're looking for 5 trades in [area] to build case studies. That's it. You get a free booking system, we get a real-world example to show others.

No contracts. Cancel anytime. Just less admin and fewer no-shows.

If you're interested, just reply "YES" and I'll book you in for a 5-minute setup call.

If not, no worries at all—just reply "NO" and I'll leave you in peace (and won't email again).

Either way, thanks for your time and good luck with the [upcoming project/season].

Cheers,
[Your Name]
```

### Call to Action

- Reply "YES" to claim free pilot
- Reply "NO" to opt out gracefully

### Success Criteria

- [ ] Clear incentive (free pilot)
- [ ] Easy yes/no response option
- [ ] Sets clear expectation: this is the last email either way

## Day 21: Remove from Active List / Nurture Loop

**Timing:** Day 21 (7 days after Day 14)

**Goal:** Either gracefully remove from active outreach OR move to long-term nurture list.

### If They Said "No" or Never Replied

Send acknowledgment email, then archive:

```
Hi [First Name],

Just a quick note—I've removed you from my active outreach list. No more emails from me on this.

If things change or you want to try AutoBookr down the line, feel free to reply to any future email or visit [autobookr_link].

Thanks for your time, and best of luck with everything.

Cheers,
[Your Name]
```

**Action:** Move to "Cold/Nurture" list in CRM. Set reminder for 90 days.

---

### If They Showed Mild Interest But Didn't Convert

Move to **nurture loop** (monthly touchpoint):

- Add to nurture list with tag: `nurture: mild-interest`
- Set calendar reminder: 30 days
- Next nurture email: Share new case study, tips, or product update

**Nurture Email Template:**

```
Hi [First Name],

Hope you're doing well. Just a quick update—

We've had some great feedback from [trade]s using AutoBookr recently. [One-line case study or testimonial].

Nothing urgent—just keeping you in the loop in case things change.

Link: [autobookr_link]

Cheers,
[Your Name]
```

---

### If They Were Hot but Didn't Book

Set reminder for 7 days, try one more channel (WhatsApp/phone call):

- Add tag: `hot-nurture`
- Day 22: Send WhatsApp message or try phone call
- If still no response: move to standard nurture

## Key Principles

1. **Value before asking** — Every email should give something useful, not just ask for a meeting.

2. **Escalating commitment** — Each touchpoint increases the ask slightly (reply → book → pilot → customer).

3. **Easy opt-out** — Always give a simple "no thanks" option. Reduces unsubscribe, improves relationship.

4. **Respect the exit** — If they say no, stop. Don't burn the bridge for future opportunities.

5. **Track everything** — Without tracking, you can't optimize. Spreadsheet minimum.

6. **Personalise** — [Trade], [Location], [Company Name] matter. Generic = ignored.
